Quick History

Table-Oriented Programming was an as then unnamed paradigm that was popular in the 80s and 90s. Common languages that used it were:

  • dBase
  • Clipper
  • Harbour/xHarbour (Open Source)
  • Visual FoxPro/Microsoft FoxPro

These languages had useful features that are not seen in modern programming languages.

With the advent of the internet, and the popularity of Object-Oriented Programming, these languages fell out of use.

In 1998-2002, as the table-oriented languages were falling out of use, "Findy Services and B. Jacobs" wrote a series of articles on "Table-Oriented Programming". These were, in their own words, unstructured and piecemeal. These advocated for various features prevalent in these languages, and a few ideas of their own.

Unfortunately, at the time, TOP was seen (especially by the biggest TOP advocate, Tablizer/TOPMind) as a competitor to OOP. This meant he spent a lot of time criticising the OOP community (often validly) for the weaknesses of OOP (and OOP was weak before modern ideas such as the invention of roles).

This led to a situation where the vast majority of people followed the OOP camp, without considering a multi-paradigm approach. Had more effort been put into integrating TOP into modern programming languages, everyone's lives would've been a lot easier (or at least most programmers).

This site is an attempt to make use of the good parts of TOP while hopefully leaving behind the oppositional nature of previous discourse on the topic.
